Sir Chandrasekhara Venkata Raman

C. V. Raman was one of the most influential and great scientists and physicists in India. He enlightened the world about the nature of light through his extensive research and discoveries throughout his life. He discovered the “Raman Effect” which fetched him the Nobel Prize in Physics in 1930. The Government of India has declared 28th February as the National Science day to honor the contribution of C V Raman and his discovery of the
“Raman Effect”.


7 November 1888

Born on November 7, in Tiruchirapalli, Madras Presidency, British India.

1902

Raman completes his high school education and enrolls in the Presidency College in Madras.

1906

His first research paper, on diffraction of light, was published in 1906 while he was still a graduate student.

1917

He was appointed the first Palit Professor of Physics by Ashutosh Mukherjee at the Rajabazar Science College under the University of Calcutta.

1926

Founded the Indian Journal of Physics in 1926

28 February 1928

The Raman effect was discovered

1930

Won the Nobel Prize in Physics "for his work on the scattering of light and for the discovery of the effect named after him."

1933

Moved to Bangalore in 1933 to become the first Indian director of the Indian Institute of Science. He founded the Indian Academy of Sciences the same year.

1948

Established the Raman Research Institute in 1948 where he worked to his last days.

1954

The Government of India honoured him with the first Bharat Ratna, its highest civilian award

21 November 1970

Died from natural causes in early morning on 21 November 1970 at the age of 82.
